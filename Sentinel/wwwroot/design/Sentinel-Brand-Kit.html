01

The mark

Geometric · Signal-driven
Concept
A single beacon, pinging outward.

The Sentinel mark is built from a single geometric unit — a rounded shield containing a concentric ping. One solid dot at the centre (the index case), three expanding rings (detection, trace, response). No ornament, no stroke weight shifts, nothing to recolour. It reads as a radar sweep, a signal, a heartbeat, and a notification — all at once.

06

Usage

Do · Don’t

Use on bone, chalk or forest backgrounds.

Mesh gradients are fine on hero surfaces.

No loud red/orange behind the mark — reserve warm colours for alerts.

Don’t rotate, recolour or swap the wordmark typeface.

07

Voice

How Sentinel speaks
Measured, never alarmed

Our users work in public health. They read carefully. Write for someone processing a case at 11pm — plain sentences, no marketing flourish, no emoji.

Precise, not technical

Use the right term (“index case”, “line listing”, “pivot”) but define it inline when space allows. Prefer specific numbers to adjectives.

Helpful in the hard moments

Outbreak screens shouldn’t shout. The interface does the serious work; the copy stays level. Lead with what the user can do next.

Honest about the alpha

This is a proof of concept. Say so. “Experimental”, “in development”, “known limitation” are on-brand words — pretending otherwise breaks trust.
